## Analyst Commentary (Claude Fable 5)
### The Big Picture

Impression share jumped from 16 percent to 27 percent this week, which is the best single-week gain in the four-week window and the one genuinely positive signal. But it came almost entirely from BH_FertWeedControl_Search, which absorbed a 301 percent spending surge and produced zero conversions, so the impression share gain bought nothing in leads. The account recorded one conversion at a CPA of $483, six times the $80 target, and the four-week conversion average is now trending down from 4.5 to 3.2. The pattern across all four weeks is the same: ad rank is losing 80-86 percent of available auctions in the converting campaigns, mobile converts at zero while desktop converts at 6-8 percent, and the two QS-3 keywords flagged in every prior report remain unresolved. The FertWeedControl campaign is now the top spender by a large margin with no conversion history to justify that position.

### Anomalies & Watch Items

- BH_FertWeedControl_Search went from $14 last week to $300 this week, a $286 increase, driven by "lawn fertilization and weed control" at $237 alone. This keyword generated 35 clicks and zero conversions. Something changed in this campaign's bidding or budget on June 18 (see Change Review below), and that change appears to have redirected a large share of the week's spend into a campaign with no conversion record.
- "mean green landscaping" spent $30 for one click. That is a competitor or branded name search, not a service search. Someone clicking that term is looking for a specific company by name, not hiring a landscaping vendor. It should be evaluated as a negative keyword candidate immediately.
- "schilllandscaping com" appeared as a waste term at $10.70. That is another competitor's website typed as a search query. This is a clear negative.
- Mobile has now produced zero conversions for four consecutive weeks while taking 70-77 percent of spend each week. Desktop has converted at 6-8 percent in three of those four weeks. At this point the mobile form or landing page is almost certainly broken or severely degraded on mobile. This is not noise.
- The 8p-12a block spent $33 with zero conversions and zero conversions in any prior week shown. There is now enough consistent data to act on this.
- "sprinkler system repair near me" dropped from QS 4 to QS 3 this week, a new low for that keyword, while BH_PC_Irrigationservice lost 86 percent of auctions to rank. These two facts are connected.
- "french drain installation fort worth" dropped one QS point to 7. Minor, but worth watching given it was at 8 last report.

### Web Dev Team Change Review

- On June 18 the team updated target_cpa_micros on BH_PC_Irrigationservice at both the campaign level and across three ad groups in a five-minute window. The specific values are not shown in the log. With spend frozen, any increase to the target CPA on this campaign conflicts with that policy and could explain unusual spend behavior. The owner should confirm what values were set and whether they align with the $80 CPA target or lower.
- On June 18 the team also updated BH_FertWeedControl_Search: two campaign-level negative keywords were created (fields show keyword.match_type and keyword.text present, so these appear structurally correct), and the campaign's target_cpa_micros and cpc_bid_ceiling_micros were both changed. The cpc_bid_ceiling_micros change is a bid cap adjustment, which is an efficiency lever and not a spend increase on its own, but combined with the target CPA change it is the most likely trigger for the $300 spend spike in that campaign this week. The actual values are not in the log, so the specific numbers cannot be reviewed. The owner should confirm what ceiling and target CPA were set.
- There is also an UPDATE CAMPAIGN_CRITERION on BH_FertWeedControl_Search with an empty fields value. An update with no fields recorded is ambiguous and could indicate a partial or failed edit. Worth asking the team what that change was intended to do.
- No keyword text was created or edited by the web dev team this week, so there are no typo, quote-in-text, or match type errors to flag on keywords.

Four straight weeks of zero mobile conversions at 70-plus percent of spend while desktop converts at 6-8 percent every week is not a bidding problem. If the form is broken or the page fails on mobile, every other action below is secondary. A working mobile form recovers the majority of weekly spend that currently produces nothing. Confidence: high

2. Add "mean green landscaping," "schilllandscaping com," "fertilizer for grass root growth," and "sprinkler system" (exact match only for the last one) as negatives this week. "Mean green landscaping" and "schilllandscaping com" are competitor name searches; people typing those are not hiring Black Hill. "Fertilizer for grass root growth" is a DIY research query. Together these four terms burned roughly $54 this week with zero conversions. Adding them stops that leak immediately and sharpens the signal quality for bidding across BH_FertWeedControl_Search and BH_PC_Irrigationservice. Confidence: high

3. Have the web dev team confirm and if necessary correct the target CPA values set on June 18 for BH_PC_Irrigationservice and BH_FertWeedControl_Search. With spend frozen, the target CPA on any campaign should not exceed $80. The FertWeedControl campaign spent $300 this week with zero conversions, and the timing matches directly with the June 18 bid changes. If the target CPA was raised above $80, reset it to $80 or lower. This is the most actionable explanation for this week's single largest spend anomaly. Confidence: high

4. Fix the landing page for "sod installation aledo tx" in BH_LeadGen_Form_Search and "lawn treatment fort worth" in BH_FertWeedControl_Search. Both have sat at QS 3 for at least five consecutive weeks. Both score High on ad relevance and Low on landing page, meaning the ad matches the search but the destination page does not. For "sod installation aledo tx," the destination page headline and opening copy must explicitly name sod installation in Aledo, TX. For "lawn treatment fort worth," the page must lead with lawn treatment in Fort Worth. A QS improvement from 3 to 6 on either keyword lowers its cost per click, improves its ad rank, and helps the campaigns win more of the auctions they currently lose. These pages need to exist by next week's report. Confidence: high

5. Apply a bid adjustment of negative 100 percent for the 8p-12a time block across all campaigns. That window has spent consistently with zero conversions across every week in the four-week dataset. Cutting it redirects roughly $33 per week into daylight hours where conversions actually occur, specifically the 12p-4p block which produced the only conversion this week. With spend frozen, this is a reallocation, not a reduction in total budget. Confidence: medium
