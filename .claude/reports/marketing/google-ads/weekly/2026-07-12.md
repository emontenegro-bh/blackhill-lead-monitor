## Analyst Commentary (Claude Fable 5)
### The Big Picture

This week is quietly one of the better ones: two leads at a $119 cost per lead, and $650 in won irrigation repair work from ads against $237 of spend, meaning ads roughly paid for themselves in closed revenue alone. The uncomfortable part is that impression share hit 12 percent, a new low for the account, and all of it is lost to ad rank, not budget, so we are being outranked in nearly nine of every ten searches we are eligible for. The cause is unchanged: Google rates almost every keyword Low on expected clickthrough rate, meaning it predicts people will skip our ads, and with spend frozen the only fix is better ads and better landing pages. Note that every won deal this week and last month came from irrigation and sprinkler repair searches, the exact terms the automated recommendations keep proposing as negatives. The $100-off promo launched July 9 with only three days of data, so its flat CTR reading means nothing yet.

### Anomalies & Watch Items

- BH_FertWeedControl_Search is missing from the spend table entirely, and the July 9 change log shows the owner changed that campaign's status. It appears paused. If intentional, fine, but it makes the long-standing "lawn treatment fort worth" landing page fix moot while it is off.
- Roughly 20 keyword status changes were made on July 6 in BH_PC_Irrigationservice with no detail on which keywords or which direction. "irrigation repair near me" and "sprinkler system repair near me" both disappeared from the quality score tracker this week, and "irrigation repair near me" was also the week's only converting search term. If that keyword was paused, we may be converting on it only through looser matching.
- The automated negative keyword list again targets core sprinkler repair terms. All three won ad deals this week were irrigation repairs. Do not apply these blindly; only "handyman sprinkler repair" is genuinely bad intent.
- The 4p-8p and 8p-12a blocks together took $114, nearly half the week's spend, with zero conversions. Across six weeks the 8p-12a window has produced exactly one conversion.
- Mobile converted for the second straight week. The "mobile is broken" theory from June is now effectively dead.
- "sprinkler system installation" shows a 33.3 percent CTR on 2 clicks. That is a low-volume artifact, not a signal.
- "Fort Worth Sprinkler Repair" is the worst headline in the account for the second consecutive week (460 impressions, 1.3 percent CTR) despite last week's recommendation to remove it.

### Priority Actions

1. Fix the ad copy in BH_PC_Irrigationservice, the highest-spend campaign, which is losing 88 percent of its auctions to ad rank because of low predicted clickthrough. Remove the headline "Fort Worth Sprinkler Repair" (worst performer two weeks running) and the description "Consistent crews & predictable service schedules..." (0.6 percent CTR). Keep and prioritize "Broken Heads Fixed Same Day," "Same-Day Sprinkler Repair," and the two diagnostic descriptions ("We walk every zone..." and "Broken valve, head, or line...") which each carry a conversion at 3.8 to 5 percent CTR. Payoff: higher CTR lifts quality score, which wins more auctions at the same frozen budget. Expect the Exp CTR column to start moving off Low within two to three weeks. Confidence: high

2. Get the list of the roughly 20 keywords whose status was changed on July 6 in BH_PC_Irrigationservice, and re-enable "irrigation repair near me" and "sprinkler system repair near me" if they were paused. Irrigation repair searches produced this week's only converting term and all three won ad deals. Payoff: protects the account's only proven revenue source. Confidence: high

3. Add "handyman sprinkler repair" as an exact-match negative in BH_PC_Irrigationservice, and skip the other six recommended sprinkler repair negatives, which are core service searches with thin one-week data. Someone searching for a handyman is not hiring a specialist irrigation crew. Payoff: small direct savings plus it prevents the automated list from cutting off the money terms. Confidence: high

4. Build the "Sod Installation in Aledo, TX" landing page for "sod installation aledo tx" in BH_LeadGen_Form_Search. This keyword has sat at quality score 3 for eight straight weeks with the landing page rated Low and ad relevance High, so the page is the confirmed weak link. The page headline and opening copy must name sod installation and Aledo explicitly, and the keyword's final URL should point there. Payoff: cheaper clicks and better position on a high-intent local search. Confidence: high

5. Exclude the 8p-12a hours via ad schedule across all active campaigns. That window spent $59 this week with zero conversions and has produced one conversion in six weeks of data. This shifts spend into 8a-12p, the block that converts most often. If a late-evening conversion appears again next week, reverse it. Payoff: the same frozen budget buys more impressions in hours that actually produce leads. Confidence: medium
