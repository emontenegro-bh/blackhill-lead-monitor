# Black Hill Landscaping - Weekly Google Ads Report
**Period**: Jun 05 - June 11, 2026

## Analyst Commentary (Claude Fable 5)
### The Big Picture
This was the worst week in over a month: one conversion, a cost per lead more than four times our $80 target, and impression share at its lowest point in the data we have. The problem is not budget, since every campaign shows 0 to 1 percent lost to budget. Our ads are losing the auction on rank, meaning Google judges our bids and ad quality too low to show us, and that got worse in all four campaigns this week. The timing matters: a large batch of keyword edits on June 5 introduced errors (detailed below) right as the reporting period began, and the campaign that was edited most heavily, BH_PC_Irrigationservice, saw one of the biggest impression share drops. The one won deal this week came from organic search, not ads.

### Anomalies & Watch Items
- Share lost to ad rank hit its worst level of the past four weeks in all four campaigns at once, while lost-to-budget stayed near zero. That pattern points to a quality or bid problem, not a spending cap.
- Mobile produced zero conversions for the second straight week despite taking most of the spend. In mid-May mobile converted well, so something changed. The mobile landing page or form is the first suspect.
- "drainage solutions fort worth" fell from a perfect quality score of 10 to 8 over two weeks. It was our best keyword on paper; worth knowing why if the slide continues.
- The 50% CTR on "commercial lawn service fort worth" is based on a couple of impressions. Too little data to mean anything.
- Roughly a third of spend ran between midnight and 8am with no conversions this week, but the May 25 week converted in those hours, so the data is not strong enough yet to justify cutting that window.

### Web Dev Team Change Review
- The team re-introduced the old typo: "irrigation istallation company near me" was created twice in BH_PC_Irrigationservice on June 5, the second time with quote marks accidentally included inside the keyword text. A correctly spelled phrase-match version was added June 8 from another account, but both typo'd versions need to be deleted.
- On June 5 the team deleted and re-created six working keywords in BH_PC_Irrigationservice (including "sprinkler system repair near me" and "sprinkler service near me") with quote characters typed into the keyword text and match type left unspecified. Typing quotes does not set phrase match when editing this way, so these keywords are likely malformed, possibly duplicated, and stripped of their performance history. This is the most likely contributor to this campaign's rank drop this week.
- The same quote-in-text, unspecified-match pattern hit "commercial landscaping near me" in BH_CommercialMaint_Search on June 5.
- On June 7 they changed the tracking template and URL suffix (the code that tags clicks for reporting) on a BH_FertWeedControl_Search ad. The log does not show the syntax, and this team has broken ValueTrack before, so it cannot be confirmed clean from the log alone. Flagged in the actions below.
- On June 9 they changed the budget and target cost-per-lead on BH_LeadGen_Form_Search. The new amounts are not in the log, so I cannot judge whether they are sensible. Worth a quick confirmation with the team on what was set and why.

### Priority Actions
1. Repair the June 5 keyword damage in BH_PC_Irrigationservice and BH_CommercialMaint_Search today: delete both typo'd "irrigation istallation company near me" keywords, delete the seven keywords created with quote marks baked into the text, and re-add each one as a proper phrase-match keyword with clean text (e.g. "sprinkler system repair near me", "commercial landscaping near me"). This attacks the rank problem directly. Expect impression share on these two campaigns to recover toward last week's levels by next report, which means more sprinkler repair calls. Confidence: high
2. Raise bids 25% on BH_CommercialMaint_Search and BH_PC_Irrigationservice. Both campaigns lose roughly 85% of available impressions to rank with essentially nothing lost to budget, so there is room to buy back position without hitting a spending cap. Payoff: more impressions and clicks on our two converting service lines this week. Confidence: high
3. Fix the landing pages behind the two quality score 3 keywords. Both "sod installation aledo tx" (BH_LeadGen_Form_Search) and "lawn treatment fort worth" (BH_FertWeedControl_Search) score High on ad relevance but Low on landing page, meaning the page they send people to does not match the search. Point "sod installation aledo tx" to a page with "Sod Installation in Aledo, TX" as the headline, and "lawn treatment fort worth" to a Fort Worth lawn treatment page, creating those pages if needed. Payoff: higher quality scores mean cheaper clicks and better ad position. Confidence: high
4. Test the mobile experience end to end this week: on a phone, click an ad from each campaign, confirm the page loads correctly, and submit the lead form. Also click-test the BH_FertWeedControl_Search ads edited June 7 to confirm the new tracking code did not break the destination URL. Two straight weeks of zero mobile conversions after strong mobile weeks in May suggests something is broken, but the data alone cannot confirm what. Payoff: if the form or URL is broken, fixing it recovers the majority of our spend that goes to mobile. Confidence: medium
5. Add [sprinkler system] as an exact-match negative keyword in BH_PC_Irrigationservice. It must be exact match only, since a phrase negative would block good searches like "sprinkler system repair." Payoff: stops a small but recurring leak of spend on a vague search that has not converted across multiple weeks. Confidence: high
