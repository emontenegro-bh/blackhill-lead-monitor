# Black Hill Landscaping - Weekly Google Ads Report

## Analyst Commentary (Claude Fable 5)
### The Big Picture

The account gave back last week's progress: one lead, a cost per lead more than three times target, and impression share stuck at 15 percent while every campaign loses 84 to 90 percent of available auctions to ad rank with nothing lost to budget. That is the same structural story as every prior week, and since spend is frozen, the only way out is quality: nearly every keyword in the account is rated Low on expected clickthrough rate, and this week's account-wide CTR of 1.9 percent was the worst in the dataset, so Google's rating is deserved. Two-thirds of spend went to the irrigation campaign on sprinkler repair searches that did not convert this week but are the account's proven money terms, so I would not read one thin week as that service line failing. One genuinely new signal: mobile produced its first conversion in six weeks, which weakens the "mobile is broken" theory that has dominated prior reports. Zero won revenue this week follows the $30,909 week, and the healthy 90-day web form total suggests pipeline lag rather than collapse.

### Anomalies & Watch Items

- The automated "What to Do" list is wrong this week. It recommends blocking "sprinkler repair", "irrigation repair near me", "sprinkler head repair near me", and "sprinkler repair grand prairie" as negatives. These are core services, and near-identical searches produced the account's only wins in June. Do not apply these.
- The week's only conversion came on mobile in the 8p-12a block. That contradicts both the five-week mobile drought and the prior recommendation to cut the 8p-12a window entirely. I am withdrawing that time-block recommendation; if it was already applied, undo it.
- CTR fell to its lowest level in the dataset and clicks fell to 37, also a low. Fewer, worse-received impressions, not a budget change, drove this.
- Two irrigation keywords dropped two quality score points each this week ("irrigation repair near me" to 3, "lawn sprinkler repair near me" to 4) right as that campaign absorbed 87 percent more spend. Worse quality plus more money is the wrong combination.
- Four keyword status changes were made in BH_PC_Irrigationservice on July 3 with no detail in the log on which keywords or which direction. See the change review.

### Web Dev Team Change Review

- The July 3 batch of roughly 35 mowing negatives in BH_FertWeedControl_Search is the right idea (residential mowing is waste per policy) but weak execution. Every negative is exact match, which only blocks that precise word string. A handful of phrase-match negatives such as "lawn mowing", "mowing service", and "mowing near me" would cover all 35 variants plus every future one. As entered, new mowing variations will keep slipping through.
- The list includes obvious paste artifacts like "lawn mowing service near me near me" and "mowing services near me near me". Harmless as negatives, but it confirms the list was dumped from a search terms export rather than built deliberately.
- Important guardrail: these mowing negatives must not be copied to BH_CommercialMaint_Search, since commercial mowing is a real service. They are currently only in the fert and weed campaign, which is correct.
- The team added a new live keyword "yard service near me" on July 3. The unspecified match type in the log is normal input normalization, not an error. The keyword choice itself is the problem: it is vague, leans residential, and debuted at quality score 3. Flagged in the actions.
- The July 3 ad edit in BH_FertWeedControl_Search changed the tracking template and URL suffix (the code that tags clicks for reporting) along with headlines. The syntax is not visible in the log and this team has broken that syntax before, so it needs a live click test.
- The June 30 negatives "sunday lawn care" (a DIY product brand) and "get rid of crabgrass" (a DIY research search) are correct calls. About 19 campaign-level criterion creations across June 30 and July 3 show no keyword text in the log, so I cannot verify their contents.
- Four keyword status updates in BH_PC_Irrigationservice on July 3 do not say which keywords were paused or enabled. Ask the team for the exact list, since that campaign carried two-thirds of this week's spend.

### Priority Actions

1. Attack the ad rank problem where the money is: in BH_PC_Irrigationservice, remove the headline "Fort Worth Sprinkler Repair" from the responsive ads (it took the most impressions of any headline this week at a 1.1 percent CTR, the worst in the account) and add two headlines that name irrigation directly, such as "Fort Worth Irrigation Repair" and "Irrigation Repair, Fixed Fast", in the ad group containing "irrigation repair near me" and "sprinkler system repair near me". Both keywords sit at quality score 3 with Low ad relevance and Low expected CTR because the ads talk about sprinklers, not irrigation. Payoff: higher CTR and quality score means winning more of the 84 percent of auctions this campaign currently loses, at the same budget. Confidence: medium

2. Do not apply the report's four sprinkler and irrigation negative keyword recommendations. Each is a core service search with only 2 to 3 clicks of data this week, and "sprinkler repair fort worth" converted in two June weeks and correlates with the $30,909 in won deals from late June. Blocking them would cut off the account's proven revenue source to save about $65 of one week's thin data. Payoff: protects the only search theme with a track record of producing paying jobs. Confidence: high

3. Build the two landing pages that have been flagged for seven straight weeks: a page headlined "Sod Installation in Aledo, TX" for "sod installation aledo tx" (BH_LeadGen_Form_Search) and a page headlined "Lawn Treatment in Fort Worth" for "lawn treatment fort worth" (BH_FertWeedControl_Search). Both keywords score High on ad relevance and Low on landing page, so the page is the confirmed weak link. Payoff: cheaper clicks and better ad position on two high-intent local searches, moving each keyword from quality score 3 toward 6. Confidence: high

4. Pause the new keyword "yard service near me" in BH_FertWeedControl_Search this week. It launched July 3, already scores quality score 3 with Low ad relevance, and "yard service" searches skew heavily toward residential mowing, which Black Hill does not offer. Payoff: stops a likely waste stream before it accumulates spend and prevents another low-score keyword from dragging the campaign's ad rank down further. Confidence: high

5. Click-test the BH_FertWeedControl_Search ad edited on July 3: on a phone, click the live ad, confirm the landing page loads at the correct URL, submit the form, and confirm the conversion records. The tracking template and URL suffix were changed by a team that has broken this syntax before, and a broken template can send clicks to error pages or drop conversion tracking silently. Payoff: protects every dollar this campaign spends and the accuracy of next week's report. Confidence: high
