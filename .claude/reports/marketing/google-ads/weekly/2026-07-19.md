## Analyst Commentary (Claude Fable 5)
### The Big Picture
On paper this is the best week in the dataset: the account hit its 10-lead target for the first time, CPA came in far under the $80 goal, and it did so on the lowest spend of any week shown. Two caveats before celebrating. First, some conversion counts look inflated, with "dfw landscaping" logging 4 conversions on a single click and "sod replacement near me" 2 on one click, so the true lead count is likely closer to 6-8 than 10. Second, nothing structural changed: impression share is still 13% against a 50% target, with 87-88% of auctions lost to ad rank and zero lost to budget, so we are converting well on a tiny slice of demand we could be winning. The single headline "Veteran-Owned & Trusted" carried all 10 conversions at a 9.4% CTR, which is the clearest ad copy signal this account has ever produced.

### Anomalies & Watch Items
- Multiple conversions recorded per single click on two search terms. This usually means duplicate form submissions or double-counting. The 10-conversion figure should be treated as directionally good but not exact.
- "Fort Worth Sprinkler Repair" is the worst headline for the third straight week (0.5% CTR on the most impressions) despite two prior recommendations to remove it. Either the July 15 ad change addressed it or the recommendation is still not executed; the report data covers days before that change, so next week will tell.
- The irrigation promo is now trending the wrong way: CTR is below the pre-promo baseline and roughly half the control campaigns' CTR two weeks in. Not yet enough volume to kill it, but this is the second consecutive weak read.
- The 8p-12a block produced 3 conversions this week. The earlier idea of cutting that window is now firmly dead, and any schedule that blocks it should be reversed.
- 8a-12p took the most spend with zero conversions this week, but it converts in other weeks. Low volume, not actionable.
- The automated negative list again targets core sprinkler repair searches. These are the terms behind most of the account's won irrigation deals. Only "handyman sprinkler repair" is genuinely bad intent.
- "landscape and yard maintenance" is the top spend keyword with zero conversions and ambiguous intent that could include residential shoppers. One more zero-conversion week and it becomes a pause candidate.
